There are a number of scripts I use on a semi-regular basis, but I hate having to do a bunch (or even a little) setup for them. I prefer the lightbox way, load the script, add some attributes to tags and you're good to go. In that spirit I recently made a little adjustment to Erik Bosrups OverLIB, which adds nice little tooltips to your page. Basically I just moved all the init stuff into the overLIB file. Here's the code:

Once that's in there you just add two attributes to your tags, rel="tooltip" and data="Title|Content". To get a title-free tooltip you put data="|Content". You can also still initialize the tooltips the old way for more advanced features I realize this is not the best solution, as it creates invalid markup and can only do two types of tooltip (at this point) but it comes in handy and it's nice to have a simple drop-in javascript file to do all this.

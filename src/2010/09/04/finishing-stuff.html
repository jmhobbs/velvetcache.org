I've been reading Scott Belsky's Making Ideas Happen: Overcoming the Obstacles Between Vision and Reality and looking at my Github account.

I start so much stuff and most of it just wastes away, partially complete. I think this is the same reason I haven't shipped any web apps, or anything beyond basic consulting work really.

No more. I've started making lists, letting things steep and trying to only do a few things at a time, and do them until they are done.

And it's working, sort of. Recently I've started and completed the following:

  • MarkedUp
  • MkLst
  • No PEAR Crypt_Blowfish
  • PanoramAh
  • UserPing for WordPress
  • Konami Unicorn Blitz

Now, none of these are huge projects, but they are done.

Totally done, feature complete as far as I am concerned.

That feels good. You should try it if you don't already.
